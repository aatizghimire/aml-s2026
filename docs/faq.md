## 📘 Frequently Asked Questions

**1. Who can enroll in this course?** 

The course is designed for security professionals, machine learning engineers, and students interested in AI security. You only need basic Python programming skills (variables, loops, functions, classes). No prior machine learning or deep learning experience is required, as Weeks 1-2 serve as a comprehensive ML crash course.

**2. How can I register for the course?** 

Registrations are not yet open. Once they are, you will be notified via email from the administration or department office. After registration opens, you can register by clicking the “Enroll Now” button on the course portal.

**3. Is there an interview process?**

Depending on the number of applications received, we may initiate a short screening process to ensure you have the prerequisite Python knowledge and the commitment required for this 10-week intensive bootcamp. Unlike other courses, there is no hardware screening, as all GPU environments are provided.

**4. Will students receive credits for this course?**

This is an intensive enrichment bootcamp and currently does not carry academic credit. However, it complements core computer science and security modules and drastically enhances employability by bridging the gap between ML engineering and cybersecurity.

**5. What tools and programming languages will be used?**

Students will gain hands-on experience entirely within Python, utilizing industry-standard open-source frameworks:

* **Deep Learning Frameworks:** PyTorch, HuggingFace
* **Adversarial & Assessment Tools:** ART (Adversarial Robustness Toolbox), TextAttack
* **Analysis & Optimization Tools:** Alibi, Optuna

**6. Will there be any certification?**

Yes. Students completing the final assessment—by scoring at least 90/100 points across the 6 timed attack notebooks—will receive a Certificate of Completion in Adversarial Machine Learning (AI Security), demonstrating practical proficiency in AI red teaming.

**7. What are the expected outcomes?**

Participants will acquire a robust understanding of machine learning vulnerabilities. You will be able to execute evasion, extraction, inversion, and poisoning attacks, test LLM-powered applications for prompt injection, and implement robust defenses like adversarial training and gradient masking.

**8. Are group projects allowed?**

Weekly workshop notebooks and code exercises are individual to ensure fundamental skill development. The Week 10 Final Assessment (comprising six distinct attack implementations) must also be completed individually to accurately assess your practical abilities.

**9. Where can I access course materials?**

All video lectures, lab notebooks, research papers, and submission portals will be made available via the course repository. Access to the pre-configured NVIDIA T4+ GPU cloud environment will be provided to enrolled students prior to Week 1. You will retain access to the course materials for 6 months.

**10. Will there be Assignments?**

Yes. There are weekly hands-on workshop notebooks requiring about 4 hours of commitment, plus 4-6 hours of self-study and reading. However, if you complete the practical attack implementations during the supported workshop hours, your take-home workload will be significantly reduced.

**11. Is attendance mandatory?**

Attendance is highly recommended for the live lectures and workshops to take advantage of TA support and office hours. Active participation is essential due to the complex, hands-on nature of building adversarial attacks from scratch.

**12. Can I withdraw from the course after enrolling?**

Withdrawals are only permitted before the first official class session (Week 1). After this period, enrollment is locked, and the student is expected to complete all coursework and the final assessment.
